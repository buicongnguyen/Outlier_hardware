Study Goal

This site collects revision material for software/hardware-engineering assessment work. The focus is three low-level engineering areas: DSP and signal processing, GPU kernel programming, and embedded firmware. Each area pairs a definitions list and core-concept notes with a bank of multiple-choice practice questions and explanations.

Signals

DSP & Signal Processing

Sampling and Nyquist, quantization, convolution, FIR/IIR filters, FFT and windowing, Z-transform, and fixed- vs floating-point.

Parallelism

GPU Kernels

SIMT execution, warps and blocks, the memory hierarchy, coalescing, occupancy, reductions, roofline thinking, CUDA/cuFFT in practice, and bottleneck hunting with profilers.

Hardware

Embedded & Firmware

Memory-mapped registers, interrupts and ISRs, DMA ping-pong, UART/SPI/I2C protocol details, bare metal vs RTOS, watchdog strategy, low-power duty cycling, and firmware profiling.

Practice format

MCQ Drills

97 questions in four formats — single answer, select-all-that-apply, step-ordering chains, and complete-the-code — from recall drills up to tagged senior/principal scenario questions, with explanations, per-section and overall scores, and a reshuffling retry button. Works offline.
